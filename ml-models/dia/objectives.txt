DIA - Drawing Indicator Analysis: Output Format

1. Emotional Expression Indicators
    - Line Pressure: Categorize as High, Normal, or Low.
    - Shading Intensity: Categorize as Heavy, Moderate, or None.
    - Overall Tone: Categorize as Dark, Balanced, or Light.

2. Space and Size Indicators
    - Page Usage: Indicate as Small (<30%), Medium, or Large (>60%).
    - Figure Size: Indicate as Small, Average, or Large.
    - Placement: Specify as Center, Side, or Corner.

3. Human Figure Structure
    - Human Figure Present: Yes or No.
    - Missing Body Parts: List any absent parts (None, Hands, Arms, Legs, Face).
    - Facial Features: Present or Absent.

4. Relational Representation (if multiple figures are present)
    - Number of Figures: Specify (2, 3, Many).
    - Distance Between Figures: Close, Moderate, or Far.
    - Self Positioning: With others or Separate.

INTERPRETATION GUIDELINES (Based on Child’s Description)

- Use only the child’s own words to explain visual features.
- Link observed drawing patterns to the child’s statements.
- Do not add emotional interpretations not mentioned by the child.
- If the child uses emotional language, reflect it gently.
- If the child’s description is neutral, keep interpretation minimal.
